Orchard Project

As an engineer I pride myself in perfect synchronicity between design and functionality. Ideally, one should never be traded for the other for great products deliver both. One project I worked on was Orchard; an everyday home hydroponics system. This product was designed to serve any small household with sustainable amounts of greens and grains. I was in charge of the electrical system which included lighting and water/nutrient controls. My teammates focused on the design, chassis dynamics and most importantly; system functionality. This intense and multi-discipline project pushed me to teach myself all about electronics, lighting and cross-system integration. I printed circuit boards and wired dozens of prototypes until we delivered a functioning hydroponics product. Below are more pictures of this product.

DotaStars

The game Dota 2 has an enormous and successful competitive scene providing prize pools of upwards to 30 million dollars. This amazing industry does a great job sustaining itself within the already established community but seems to have difficulty in communicating to newcomers. Having all the match and tournament schedules centralized in one location would enable more viewers to commit to the year-round competitive scene. For this reason I began developing this DotaStars application which will eventually allow users to favorite and follow particular matches, tournaments, teams or even players. This would allow users to more easily commit to viewing and supporting the competitive scene.

The current state of this app is intended to demonstrate my comprehensive ability with Ruby on Rails. I implemented dynamic elements via javascript and implemented as many DRY practices as possible; almost every page uses a partial and there was significant effort put into simplifying the SCSS. A significant challenge was allowing the user to change the team roster all at once instead of having to edit each player individually. Further development will enable admins and users to manipulate information. Admins will be allowed to edit their teams while users will have the ability to favorite and follow events, matches and teams. Note; my visual graphics skills aren't up to par with my coding as my goal but I intend to eventually involve graphic designers in the project.
